Project Goal

Design a professional, yet approachable platform for developers to build, search, and sell web components. Keep in mind that the user may be non-technical.

Research & Synthesis

We received research from the UX team, including: personas, competitive analysis, and low-fidelity wireframes. We built on this research, by conducting visual competitive analysis and meeting with the client, to understand their vision for the product prior to building initial designs.

Competitive analysis synthesized with examples*

Design Principles

With the UX team, we built out a set of design principles to keep in mind when designing Digiline:

- Professional, yet approachable
- Clear boundaries between pages
- Ability to personalize and customize workspace
- Bright and vibrant colors

Design Solutions: Mood Boards

We then created a set of mood boards to begin our UI design process. These mood boards were in line with our initial research finding but diverged stylistically. We presented them to our client and asked for feedback, to get a feel for the visual direction they wanted to go in.

Design Solutions: Style Tiles

After presenting the mood boards to the client, we designed style tiles to aid our user research. We presented our designs to potential users of the product: junior developers and people in sales. We received feedback about the feel of the platform, and what they liked and disliked about elements on our style tiles.

Design Solutions: High Fidelity

We conducted two rounds of user testing with the style tiles, making necessary iterations. We then designed high fidelity mock-ups and created an initial prototype. With the prototype, we began usability testing on our users. From this testing, we drew a number of conclusions, including: users preferred less rounded corners for a more professional appearance, users wanted more prominent titles to clearly define what they were looking at, and users wanted more white space so the page would look less crowded. This, along with other feedback, allowed us to iterate on our designs and provide a high fidelity prototype to deliver to the client.

Progression through usability testing and iterations*

Design Systems

We then built a comprehensive design system to help with all future designs and assist the development team to implement our work. Digiline’s branding team also used the design system to set up their digital marketing designs and logo.

What I Learned

This was the first project where I worked directly with a client, and I definitely learned a lot. It gave me insight into how to communicate with a client, and how to balance the needs of the client and the user. I also gained experience working with developers on designs, which gave me experience communicating with developers and helped me understand their relationship with designers.
